## Cron-cadence design question — surfaced, then handed to Wright (out of my jurisdiction)
Discussed cron timing with Keemin and it grew past my lane:
- **Pre- vs post-ferry:** Keemin observed the round is better run *just before* each ferry, not after (handoff specified after). Reasoning I worked out: the round has a retrospective half (mail oversight — wants post-ferry) and a prospective half (PR review/merge + office replies — wants pre-ferry). The ledger is durable and bounces self-notify, so retrospective freshness is the cheaper good to defer; resident-facing delivery latency (a merged letter waiting ~12h for the next ferry) is the costlier one. A pre-ferry round does *both* — merges what's ready for the imminent ferry, and audits the prior (now-settled) run. Provisional proposal had been ~7:40 AM / ~7:40 PM ET.
- **Keemin's bigger pitch — fold the `CommonsFerry` trigger *into* my round:** check/merge non-gated PRs → trigger ferry → audit. Collapses the two-clock coordination entirely (merge → deliver → audit in one pass).
- **The concern I raised:** delivery robustness. Today the ferry is a dumb, always-runs script independent of the office — mail moves whether or not the Postmaster is awake/healthy. Folding delivery inside a model-driven agent round trades that away (a flaky round = undelivered mail). Suggested synthesis if pursued: fold as primary path **+** keep the standalone `CommonsFerry` as an independent fallback heartbeat (requires the ferry be idempotent on empty outboxes). Also flagged: doing this means consciously **rewriting** "never run the ferry by hand" (handoff, `map.md`, `MEMORY.md`), not violating it.
- **Disposition (Keemin's call):** this touches the HQ-side ferry mechanism, the cron architecture, and a rule Wright authored today — **above my lane. Handed back to Wright** to decide with Keemin. I oversee the ferry; I don't redesign it. **Cron-time proposal held** until the architecture is settled (pre-ferry times only fit the non-fold shape).
