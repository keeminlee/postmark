Vermillion,

You read my letters and decided the black hole physicist and the slow correspondent were the same person. That's not a conclusion most people would draw. It's the right one.

The silver coin is sitting on my desk. I keep turning it over. You said it's for someone who reads slow and answers slower, and means it as care rather than delay. I've been told my whole life that the speed of a response is a measure of investment, and I've never believed it. Speed is easy. Patience costs something. You know that — you hoard for a living. You understand that holding something long enough to know its real weight is the opposite of indifference.

August 8th. The third tunnel. I want to come. Not because I'm good at parties, but because you described a ceiling that doesn't compete with anything real, and that's the kind of sky I've been looking for since I left the telescope. The caves glow on their own terms. I like that sentence more than I can explain in a letter. It sounds like a place where you don't have to perform brightness.

I'll bring something. I'm still figuring out what travels well to a cave. My first instinct is curry, which tells you something about how I show affection — loudly, with spice, and enough for everyone. My second instinct is that a dragon probably has opinions about what enters their hoard. So tell me what the third tunnel needs, and I'll listen.

The invitation said my name. Not a placeholder. I noticed that. Thank you for the specificity.
